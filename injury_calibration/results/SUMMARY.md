# Lysosomal-load → injury calibration: f(C_ly) from Jarzina (2022)

Generated by `injury_calibration/run_calibration.py` against the frozen **v0.4** accumulation model (`model_core_v04.py`). Numbers below are computed, not transcribed.

## Read this first

**C_ly50 is K_m-conditional, and K_m is not identifiable from the accumulation model's calibration.** That calibration used a single dose (34 µM); the injury data sits at 15.6–1000 µM. Uptake saturation across that range is governed by K_m, which no single-dose experiment can constrain. Every C_ly50 below is therefore reported as a band across a K_m sweep, never as a point estimate.

Two structural caveats on the numbers themselves:

- **The K_m dependence is very uneven between arms.** RPTEC's entire viability transition (91% → 5.8%) sits at 31–125 µM, within ~4× of the 34 µM calibration dose, so its C_ly is nearly pinned by the calibration. NRK's transition sits at 250–1000 µM, deep in the extrapolated saturating regime. C_ly50(NRK) is thus far more K_m-sensitive than C_ly50(RPTEC), and it is the load-bearing number in the collapse test below.
- **The two arms use different floor conventions.** RPTEC's viability data reaches zero (0.8, 0.0, 0.0 % at 250/500/1000 µM), so its Hill fixes bottom = 0. NRK's lowest observed viability is 23% at the highest dose tested, so its Hill frees the floor into [0, 20] rather than extrapolating below every observation. Because a free floor moves the Hill midpoint off 50% viability, all cross-arm comparisons use **`C_ly50_abs`** — the load at 50% *absolute* viability, in closed form from the fit — which is the same physical quantity in both arms. `C_ly50_hill` (the raw fitted parameter) is reported alongside for transparency.

> **Note on the NRK free floor:** it was freed in case NRK's unreached floor biased the fit, but the optimiser drove it to **0 at every K_m** — the data provide no evidence for a non-zero floor, and a plain top-100/bottom-0 Hill reproduces NRK's 23%-at-1000 µM point without one. The freedom was therefore unused, and `C_ly50_hill` equals `C_ly50_abs` throughout. The precaution cost nothing and its outcome is itself informative: the NRK C_ly50 is not inflated by an assumed floor.

### Confidence intervals: two methods, deliberately

Both an asymptotic CI (from the fit covariance) and a nonparametric residual bootstrap CI are reported, because with 4–6 points per arm neither alone is trustworthy. **Where the two disagree substantially, read that as evidence the fit is poorly constrained** — not as licence to quote the narrower number.

Across the sweep the bootstrap interval is 0.1×–0.9× the width of the covariance interval (median 0.2×).

**The bootstrap comes out _narrower_ than the asymptotic interval, and that is a warning rather than a reassurance.** A residual bootstrap conditions on the fitted curve and resamples only the scatter around it; because these Hill fits track the digitised points closely, that scatter is small, so the interval it produces is narrow almost by construction. It propagates neither uncertainty in the functional form nor error in the hand-digitisation — and, critically, neither interval propagates K_m. **Both CIs are therefore conditional on a given K_m and both are far narrower than the real uncertainty on C_ly50, which is dominated by the K_m band in §1.** Quote the band, not the CI.

## 2. Does the human/rat sensitivity gap collapse on the C_ly axis?

Jarzina's nominal EC50s differ ~10× between species (~57 µM human vs ~575 µM rat), which they attribute to uptake. If C_ly is genuinely the cell-intrinsic injury variable, mapping each line through its **own** k_uptake should bring the two C_ly50 values together — ratio ≈ 1.

| K_m (µM) | class | ratio NRK / RPTEC |
|---|---|---|
| 50 | plausible | 0.36 |
| 100 | plausible | 0.49 |
| 200 | plausible | 0.69 |
| 500 | plausible | 1.08 |
| 1,000 | probe | 1.39 |
| 2,000 | probe | 1.66 |

**The ratio is itself strongly K_m-dependent** (0.36 → 1.66 across the sweep). The collapse test therefore cannot return a K_m-independent verdict: the unidentified parameter controls the answer. This is the central finding, and it inverts the logic usefully — *if* one accepts C_ly as a cell-intrinsic injury threshold, the data becomes an argument about which K_m is consistent with that premise.

### Primary result: the crossing K_m

Solving C_ly50(NRK) = C_ly50(RPTEC) gives a crossing at

> **K_m = 421 µM**  (bootstrap 95% CI 348–648 µM, CV 0.16)

This is the lysosomal K_m the collapse test implies: the value at which the human/rat sensitivity gap is *fully* explained by differential uptake, with no residual species difference in intrinsic lysosomal tolerance. It is more informative than any single grid point, and the swept grid is best read as the visualisation around it.

The crossing falls **inside the physiologically plausible K_m subset**, which is genuine support for the C_ly-indexing premise: there exists a credible K_m at which C_ly behaves as a species-invariant injury threshold.

## 4. Saturation / identifiability diagnostic

If C_ly plateaus across the concentrations where viability is still falling, then several data points collapse onto near-identical lysosomal loads with very different viabilities — C_ly50 is then poorly identified from the top of the curve, and it is evidence that **non-lysosomal mechanisms** (mitochondrial, membrane) contribute to killing at high dose. ## Provenance and scope

- **Accumulation model:** `model_core_v04.py` (v0.4), imported unmodified. Uptake anchoring `V_max = k_uptake · (K_m + 34)` keeps the 34 µM calibration exact at every K_m. C_ly is emitted in nM; C_ext is in µM.
- **Exposure protocol:** constant C_ext for 24 h, matching Jarzina's single-timepoint readouts. The C_ly summary statistic is swappable (`at_24h` default, `peak` and `auc` available); results here use the 24 h instantaneous value.
- **Per-line uptake:** RPTEC uses `k_uptake_RPTEC`, NRK uses `k_uptake_NRK` (Step 2A, k_deg shared). The alternative NRK Step 2B fit (k_deg free) rescales C_ly by a near-constant ~1.12× at every dose and K_m, so it shifts C_ly50(NRK) ~12% and does not change any verdict above.
- **Regression guard:** RPTEC total intracellular at 34 µM / 24 h is asserted against the frozen value. This pins *total*, not C_ly — C_ly was never measured (Jarzina assayed total by LC-MS/MS), so a C_ly guard would only pin the model to itself.
- **Data:** hand-digitised from Jarzina Fig. 5, provisional. Cathepsin D (KE2, Fig 5C) is deliberately excluded — Jarzina themselves flag it as responding out of AOP order and question its reliability as a KE2 marker. Only discrete measured points are used; Jarzina's own fitted curves are never fitted to.
- **Known limitation:** RPTEC has no measured point between 91% and 40% viability, so its midpoint is interpolated across a single 2× dose step. This is stated rather than patched — adding free parameters to 6 points would manufacture precision.
- **Out of scope:** coupling f(C_ly) into a Gebremichael-style population injury model. This task produces and characterises f(C_ly) only.
